A postpartum doula is a continuation in care

Bringing a doula into your home to support your family during the first days and weeks of a newborn's life can be transformational during a time of great change and adjustment.

Midwives and OBs are critical to maternal and neonatal health, and hiring a postpartum doula extends that knowledgable care into the early days when you need reassurance-and a warm meal!-to know everyone is thriving.

Milk Moon Doula & Breastfeeding Support is run by Kate Rutledge, a three-time mom herself, and trained lactation peer counselor. You can learn more about the services she provides, or get in touch if you're ready to chat today.

Who is Kate Rutledge?

Kate is the postpartum doula at Milk Moon, and a 3-time mom herself. As a trained counselor with Nursing Mother's Counsel, she has in-depth experience counseling families with complex breastfeeding situations, as well as over seven years of her own breastfeeding adventures.

You can contact Kate to set up a meeting, or just chat, to see if she and your family are a fit.

What is a postpartum doula?

A postpartum doula is a woman who comes to your home and helps during the transition after a new baby (or babies!) arrives. She is there to answer questions, ease concerns, and aid with basic household, family, and infant care.
